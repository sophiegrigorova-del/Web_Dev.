Percy Jackson and the Lightning Thief

Rick Riordan
Percy Jackson and the Lightning Thief follows twelve-year-old Percy Jackson as he discovers he is a demigod, the son of Poseidon, and is accused of stealing Zeus' master lightning bolt. To prevent a war among the gods, Percy, accompanied by his friends Annabeth and Grover, goes on a cross-country quest to find the true thief, battling mythical creatures and journeying to the Underworld, before returning the bolt and proving his innocence.

About the Author

Rick Riordan is a best-selling American author of children's books, best known for the Percy Jackson & the Olympians series, which blends Greek mythology with modern settings. A former middle school teacher, he created the Percy Jackson universe as bedtime stories for his son, who has ADHD and dyslexia. His books, including the Kane Chronicles (Egyptian mythology) and Magnus Chase series (Norse mythology), are known for their engaging plots, humor, and exploration of diverse mythologies for young readers.
