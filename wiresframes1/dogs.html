Oxytocin—known variously as the “love hormone” and the “cuddle hormone”—is released by dog brains, too. It enables them to fall in love with both dogs and humans, and you are not one to question their lifestyle choices nor whom they deign to love.

According to canine-intelligence expert Stanley Coren, “They can also deliberately deceive, which is something that young children only start developing later in their life.”

A 2003 study from The Veterinary Record revealed that merely petting a dog infected with a parasitic roundworm known as Toxocara canis can transmit the parasite to humans, at which point the roundworm can nestle in the back of your eyeball and eventually make you go blind. So…pet with caution and wash your hands.

Dogs are domesticated canids, scientifically known as Canis lupus familiaris, descended from wolves over 15,000 years ago. They are known for their strong sense of smell, ability to learn over 100 words, and diverse breeds that vary widely in size and appearance. Most pet dogs have an average lifespan of 10–13 years.

Golden Retrievers were developed in Scotland in the 19th century to retrieve game. They are intelligent, friendly, affectionate, and have an average lifespan of 10–12 years.

Beagles are friendly, vocal scent hounds with a white-tipped tail, powerful noses, and a stubborn yet food-motivated personality. They are used as working dogs and were popularized by Snoopy.

Corgis are small herding dogs with short legs, long bodies, and large upright ears. There are two distinct breeds: the Pembroke and the Cardigan Welsh Corgi.

The Labrador Retriever is a medium-to-large sporting dog bred in Newfoundland to assist fishermen. Friendly, loyal, and versatile, Labs are among the most popular family pets worldwide.
